Activity 1 — Tidal Power Plant (suitable for a coastal village)

Objective: Prepare the architecture of a tidal power plant sized to serve a coastal village (approx. few 100's kW up to 1–2 MW depending on resource).

Design notes: For small communities, tidal-stream turbines (in-channel arrays) give a good balance of cost and environmental impact. Key components: turbine array, subsea inter-array cables, subsea-to-shore export cable, onshore substation (protection & step-up), distribution to village or local microgrid, and maintenance/anchoring systems.

Quick sizing example (first-order)

Assumptions: mean current speed = 2.5 m/s, turbine swept area per unit = 20 m², power coefficient Cp≈0.35, water density ρ=1025 kg/m³.

  • Single turbine power ≈ 0.5 × ρ × A × Cp × V³ = 0.5×1025×20×0.35×(2.5³) ≈ 28 kW.
  • To get ~500 kW installed → need ≈ 18 turbines (allow extra for array losses and maintenance → design for 20 units).
  • Estimated annual energy (with capacity factor ≈ 30%) → 500 kW × 0.30 × 8760 ≈ 1.3 GWh/year — suitable for a village.

Reporting points

Resource assessment (tidal range/current profile), array spacing (to avoid wake losses), subsea cable sizing, environmental impact, O&M plan, and simple cost/benefit estimates.

Activity 2 — Biomass-based Biogas System for 100 Cattle

Objective: Design a biogas generation system for a dairy farm handling 100 cattle (manure feedstock base).

Design notes: Typical system includes manure collection (floor scrapers or flush), pre-treatment (screening, mixing with water if needed), anaerobic digester (CSTR/plug-flow), gas storage, gas utilization (CHP or gas cookstoves), and digestate handling as fertilizer.

Sample calculations (first-order)

Assumptions (typical values): fresh manure per cow ≈ 40 kg/day, total solids (TS) ≈ 8%, volatile solids (VS) ≈ 70% of TS, biogas yield ≈ 0.4 m³/kg VS.

  • Total fresh manure/day = 100 × 40 = 4000 kg/day.
  • Total solids (TS) ≈ 4000 × 0.08 = 320 kg/day.
  • VS ≈ 320 × 0.70 = 224 kg VS/day.
  • Estimated biogas ≈ 224 × 0.4 ≈ 89.6 m³/day (≈ 64 m³ CH₄/day if CH₄≈70%).
  • Energy content (CH₄ ~ 35.8 MJ/m³) → ~64×35.8 ≈ 2291 MJ/day ≈ 636 kWh/day of chemical energy (thermal). Electrical from small engine (efficiency ~30%) → ~190 kWh/day electrical.

Reporting points

Specify digester type, HRT (e.g., 30–40 days for mesophilic), operating temp (mesophilic ~35°C), slurry handling, safety & gas cleanup if using engines, expected O&M, and a short payback estimate if CHP is used.

Activity 3 — Geothermal Power Plant (hot water wells)

Objective: Design a geothermal power plant system for electricity generation where hot water wells exist (binary OR flash system depending on temperature).

Design notes: For moderate-temperature wells (80–170°C) an organic Rankine cycle (binary) is preferred. For >180°C, flash-steam may be used. Include production well, separators/heat exchangers, turbine, condenser (air/water cooled), reinjection wells, and balance-of-plant.

Example sizing notes (first-order)

Assumptions: hot water production = 50 kg/s at 120°C, reinjection temp target ~40°C, binary cycle thermal efficiency ~10–12% (depends on temperatures).

  • Thermal power = mass × Cp × ΔT ≈ 50 kg/s × 4.18 kJ/kg·K × (120−40)K ≈ 16720 kW thermal (~16.7 MWth).
  • Electrical output (at 11% efficiency) ≈ 16.7 MWth × 0.11 ≈ 1.84 MWe — suitable for village-scale or small community supply.
  • Choose working fluid for ORC (e.g., R245fa, pentane variants) based on critical temp and environmental considerations.

Reporting points

Include reservoir temperature and flow tests, choice of ORC working fluid, cooling method, reinjection strategy, environmental assessment (induced seismicity, water chemistry), and an outline O&M schedule.
